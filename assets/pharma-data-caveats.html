Understanding the data landscape
Rx
Pharmacy Claims
Prescription fill data captured at the pharmacy point-of-sale via switch networks (RelayHealth/McKesson, Change Healthcare). Captures retail, mail, and specialty fills. Strongest for oral and self-administered drugs.
Misses: buy-and-bill, in-office infusions, hospital outpatient, uninsured patients, VA/IHS dispensing.
Mx
Medical Claims
ICD-10 diagnosis, CPT procedure, and J-code drug administration claims submitted by providers to payers for reimbursement. Essential for buy-and-bill infusion products (oncology, neurology, rare disease).
Misses: uninsured patients, self-pay, non-covered services, clinical nuance (no lab values or biomarkers).
867
Channel / 867 Data
EDI 867 Product Transfer and Resale data flows from wholesaler to manufacturer, tracking unit movements from distributor to pharmacy or provider. Foundation of specialty pharmacy and GTN analytics.
Often blinded: first 3 digits of ZIP only, weekly aggregates, direct sales excluded, 340B/GPO visibility varies.
EHR
Electronic Health Records
Structured clinical data (diagnoses, labs, vital signs, notes) from provider EHR systems (Epic, Cerner, Allscripts). Richest clinical context — but coverage is fragmented across health systems.
Coverage is non-random; biased toward academic centers and specific geographies. No coverage outside network.
SP
Specialty Pharmacy Data
Hub, enrollment, dispense, adherence, and refill data from specialty pharmacies (Accredo, CVS Specialty, Walgreens Specialty, AllianceRx). Closest to patient-level truth for specialty biologics.
Access requires manufacturer contracting. Data sharing restrictions vary. Excluded SPs create blind spots.
Dist
Distributor / Ex-Factory
Sales from manufacturer to wholesaler (McKesson, AmerisourceBergen, Cardinal Health). Upstream of patient demand; influenced by stocking behavior. Used for GTN accruals and inventory pipeline.
Does not equal patient demand. Inventory build/drawdown creates demand signal distortion. Not patient-linked.
EHR
Electronic Health Records
Structured clinical data (diagnoses, vitals, labs, unstructured notes) from provider EHR systems (Epic, Cerner, Allscripts/Veradigm, Practice Fusion). Richest clinical context but fragmented across health systems.
Coverage is non-random; biased by EHR vendor market share, practice type, and geography. No cross-network visibility.
Lab
Clinical Lab / Diagnostics
Biomarker values, diagnostic test results, and test orders from national reference labs (Quest, LabCorp) or in-house hospital lab systems. Critical for precision medicine and biomarker-driven population identification.
Reference labs miss in-house hospital lab results. Test orders ≠ results. Reference ranges vary by lab and instrument across vendors.
NGS
Genomics / Multi-Omic
Next-generation sequencing panel data, tumor biomarker profiles, ctDNA liquid biopsy, and pharmacogenomic results from Tempus, Foundation Medicine, Caris, Guardant. Foundation of precision oncology and targeted therapy development.
Academic center bias; FFPE quality failures are non-random; panel gene coverage differs; somatic vs. germline classification errors possible.
Rpt
Analyst Reports / Forecasts
Secondary research, market forecasts, pipeline intelligence, and formulary tracking from intelligence vendors (Clarivate/DRG, Evaluate, GlobalData, Norstella/MMIT, Citeline). Used for competitive intelligence, market sizing, and BD&L valuation.
Consensus forecasts have documented optimism bias. Reports lag real-time events. Methodology not always transparent. Updates on 1–2 year cycles.
